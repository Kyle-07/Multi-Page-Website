The Parts Of A Computer

The Graphics Card

The Graphics Card, also known as the Graphics Proccesing Unit, or GPU is a very important part of a computer. It handles the rendering of graphics. It is necessary in order for a compter to output an image, and for running graphically intensive games. It is also used for video editing, crypto mining, and 3d rendering and animation. Some GPUs are integrated into the CPU. These GPUs are called iGPUs, and they are common on lower end computers. iGPUs are generally not very powerful. GPUs are mainly manufactured by Nvidia and AMD, but Intel has began to develop its own discrete (not integrated into cpu) GPU

The Processor

The processor, also known as the Central Processing Unit is the brain of a computer. It is responsible for carrying out the majority of the calculations and instructions necessary for the system to function. The CPU retrieves data and instructions from the computer's memory and executes them to perform tasks such as running programs, managing input and output operations, and controlling the flow of information within the system. The CPU also contains a control unit that directs the operations of other components in the computer system, including input/output devices, memory, and the arithmetic and logic units. Modern desktop CPUs are usually from either Intel, known as Team Blue, or AMD (Advanced Micro Devices), known as Team Red

The CPU cooler

The function of a CPU cooler is to dissipate the heat generated by the CPU during operation. The CPU generates heat as it processes data and performs computations. If this heat is not dissipated properly, it can cause the CPU to overheat, which can lead to damage and potentially system failure. A CPU cooler works by transferring the heat from the CPU to a heatsink, which is a metal plate with fins that increase its surface area. The heatsink is then cooled by a fan, which blows air over the fins, dissipating the heat into the surrounding environment. There are several types of CPU coolers available, including air coolers and liquid coolers. Air coolers use fans to cool the heatsink, while liquid coolers use a liquid coolant to transfer the heat away from the CPU and dissipate it via a radiator. Pictured on the left is an AIO Liquid Cooler, and on the right is a single tower air cooler

The Memory

RAM (Random Access Memory) is a type of computer memory that is used to temporarily store data that the CPU needs to access quickly. The function of RAM is to provide fast, short-term storage space for data that is currently being used by the computer's operating system (OS), applications, and processes. When a computer is turned on, the OS and other software programs are loaded into the RAM from the computer's storage. The CPU then accesses data from RAM as needed, rather than from the slower storage drive. This allows the computer to operate much faster and more efficiently, as it can quickly retrieve and process data. The amount of RAM installed in a computer determines how much data can be stored in it at any given time. The more RAM a computer has, the more programs and data it can handle simultaneously without slowing down. This is important for video editing software or video games, which require a lot of memory to run smoothly.

The Motherboard

The motherboard is the main circuit board of a computer, and it serves as the central hub that connects and controls all of the computer's components. The function of a motherboard is to provide a platform for all of the computer's hardware components to communicate with each other and with the CPU . The motherboard contains a variety of essential components, such as the CPU socket, memory (RAM) slots, expansion slots (for GPUs, among other things), and connectors for peripheral devices such as storage, optical drives, and USB ports. These components are interconnected by a network of traces and circuits, which allow them to communicate with each other and with the CPU. The motherboard also contains a chipset, which acts as a bridge between the CPU and the other components on the motherboard. The chipset provides a set of instructions to the CPU that help it to communicate with the other hardware components on the motherboard. It is essentially the backbone of the computer, providing the necessary connections and controls to make everything work together as a cohesive system.

The Power Supply

The function of a power supply unit (PSU) is to convert AC (alternating current) power from the electrical outlet into DC (direct current) power that is used by the computer's components. The PSU provides a stable and reliable source of power to the various hardware components in the computer. The power supply is responsible for providing the correct voltage and current to each component, ensuring that they receive the power they need to operate properly. It also regulates the voltage to prevent any fluctuations that could damage the components. A high-quality psu is crucial for maintaining the stability and longevity of a computer.In addition, PSUs can be modular. Modular PSUs allow the user to only connect the cables that are needed for their specific system configuration, reducing cable clutter and improving airflow.

The Storage

There are several types of computer storage drives. One of them is the Hard Disk Drive (HDD). A hard disk drive is a magnetic storage device that uses spinning disks to store data. It is a common type of storage drive used in computers and offers large storage capacities at lowwer costs. It is able to do this as the speeds are slow.HDDs connect to the motherboard by SATA. A HDD is pictured on the right Another type is a Solid State Drive (SSD). A solid-state drive uses flash memory to store data, making it faster and more reliable than a HDD. They connect to the motherboard via a SATA cable. SSDs are increasingly popular due to their faster data transfer speeds, lower power consumption, and improved durability. However, they cost more. A SSD is shown in the middle. Additionally, another type is M.2 NVME. Technically, an NVME drive is a type of SSD, but they look quite different as you can see from the image. An NVME screws directly into the motheboard, rather than using a SATA cable like a HDD or SSD. They can reach faster speeds than a SSD because of this.

The Case

The function of a PC case (also known as a computer case or chassis) is to provide a protective enclosure for the internal components of a computer system, as well as to help manage the flow of air to keep the components cool. The case also provides a platform for the motherboard. A PC case typically includes a number of features to support the installation and management of the internal components, such as drive bays for hard drives and optical drives, expansion slots for additional cards, and cable management features to keep the cables organized and out of the way. In addition to providing physical protection and organization for the internal components, the PC case also helps to manage the flow of air to keep the components cool. A good PC case will have ventilation holes or fans to provide adequate airflow, and may also include filters to keep dust and debris from accumulating inside the case.

The Fans

The function of PC fans is to help keep the internal components of a computer system cool by moving air through the case and over the components. Computer components generate a lot of heat, and if this heat is not dissipated effectively, it can cause the components to overheat, which can result in system instability, damage to the components, or even system failure. PC fans work by pulling cooler air into the case and pushing hot air out. This helps to maintain a consistent airflow over the components, preventing hot spots from developing and ensuring that heat is efficiently dissipated. Fans are typically located at the front and rear of the case, as well as on the CPU cooler and GPU.
